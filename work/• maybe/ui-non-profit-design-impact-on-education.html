Home Work

Impact on Education Non-Profit Website

It all started with the platform's shift in focus from transactional (tVOD) sales to a subscription (sVOD) model that ended up setting a big course change in place for the outdated themes.

Introduction

My Role

Besides designing, developing and maintaining themes, I also lead the UI/UX efforts and continue to improve the customer’s content discovery, purchasing and consumption experience. I work in close collaboration with the CTO and the Customer Success team to keep improving the themes.

The Challenge

...ready, ...set, ...HOLD ON?!

Though the number of existing themes were far too few, it was not the platform's biggest bottleneck.

The themes:

  • were not versatile enough
  • were difficult to customize
  • were difficult to maintain
  • were behind on the trends
  • had no commonality between them
  • took too long to develop, 9-12 months

The Approach

First things, first.

First the development and maintenance cost of the themes needs to come down dramatically before starting the design of any new themes. During competitor research we started looking at the big players in the website theme space for the industry standards, and it is there where we started looking at the idea of implementing a Parent/Child type theme structure.

So how do we get there?

Simplify, simplify!

  1. audit existing themes for critical errors
  2. fix all bugs in active themes and retire all legacy themes
  3. move clients off legacy themes
  4. streamline templates
  5. develop master theme structure

Design & Testing

Predictable and Familiar

By standardizing the pages that are involved with finding, purchasing, consuming content and account management the Master User Flow is established. This user flow honors the principles of Nielsen’s Heuristics to ensure a pleasant, predictable experience for the user.

If this, than this, if that, than that. Also known as formulas

There are different ways clients can sell their content on the themes, such offering content for sale, rent, and subscription. Then, they can specify how they want to handle pre-sale access. The variations have been established in the Master User Flow on what behavior will be triggered based on the pricing and delivery preferences specified by the client.

Pricing Models

  • Transactional (tVOD): Rent, one-time purchase.
  • Subscription (tVOD): Subscribe on a monthly or yearly basis.
  • Both (tVOD + sVOD): Rent, one-time purchase or subscribe on a monthly or yearly basis

Content Delivery Models

  • Default: Users can browse the library before purchase.
  • Log In Required: Users can not see any library contents before purchase.
  • Members: Theme handles the delivery of the content only, users are required to log in.

With the new structure in place we can now focus on developing effective themes, focused on conversion and reducing churn in subscribers.

Testing Performance

User Testing

During a User Task design meeting with the CTO, COO and a Customer Success Manager we brainstormed on the tasks a user would try to complete on the themes or what support tickets have been coming in specifically?

During the User Tests we want to know:

  • Can a user complete the tasks given?
  • How does it perform against a similar platform?
  • What is a user expecting to find?
  • Does the user feel confident using the platform?

Conversions

While some themes are converting as high as 3% I am currently leading an effort to put A/B tests in place to improve these conversion rates.

So far we have found that these numbers are highly influenced by the following factors:

  • What theme is it?
  • What client is it?
  • How good is their (external) marketing effort?
  • How good is their price point?
